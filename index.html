How it works

Three stages, three cash releases

Capital is released to you at three points in the supply chain — with no invoices and no direct billing.

1

You upload inventory

Fero takes legal title to your stock and immediately advances cash against 60% of the ExWorks value — before a single case has sold.

Cash released immediately
2

Distributor is invoiced

When a distributor picks up stock, Fero releases a further advance equal to 55% of the FOB invoice value, net of what was advanced at upload.

Cash released on invoice
3

Distributor pays Fero

Once the distributor settles (typically within 2 months), Fero remits the remaining balance after deducting all fees and recharge costs.

Final settlement paid

Fee structure

Three fees. Always net-settled.

All fees are deducted from your proceeds on distributor settlement — never billed directly, preserving your cash flow.

Upload fee

1%

A one-off charge on the funded value when Fero takes title to your inventory. Charged once per unit at upload.

Charged at upload

Sale fee

≤10%

Applied to the FOB invoice value when stock is sold to your distribution network. Rate can reduce based on credit quality and volume.

Charged on sale

Monthly fee

SOFR+1%

Charged monthly on the outstanding funded balance. Rate moves with SOFR — a falling rate directly reduces your cost.

Charged monthly

Recharge costs — not Fero fees

Third-party costs paid upfront by Fero are recharged at actual cost on final settlement. These are pass-throughs, not markups. Estimated ~£28.50/case in the illustrative model.
